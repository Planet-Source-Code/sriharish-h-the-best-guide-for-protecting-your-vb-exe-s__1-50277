1: The basic way to protect your exe is to mask the strings. Strings are the stuff which you place it in quotes while writing a program. These strings are the keys to the hackers, by using a diassembler they can easily see your strings. You can try this by installing any diassembler like PE Explorer: www.heaventools.com or Win32Dasm. These are powerful legal programs which can extract strings from a PE Format file. PE is Portable Executable file. Without string masking your commercial application is no way protected. Now, you know why even a single copy of your Shareware software is not selling.

Unprotected Applications: Message Boxes shown after application is registered. Now, the String in the quotes can be ripped by Diassembler.

Msgbox "Thank you for registering this software", VbInformation

Protected Applications:

Msgbox Chr(84) & Chr(104) & Chr(97) & Chr(110) & Chr(107) & Chr(32) & Chr(121) & Chr(111) & _
Chr(117) & Chr(32) & Chr(102) & Chr(111) & Chr(114) & Chr(32) & Chr(114) & Chr(101) & _
Chr(103) & Chr(105) & Chr(115) & Chr(116) & Chr(101) & Chr(114) & Chr(105) & Chr(110) & _
Chr(103) & Chr(32) & Chr(116) & Chr(104) & Chr(105) & Chr(115) & Chr(32) & Chr(115) & _
Chr(111) & Chr(102) & Chr(116) & Chr(119) & Chr(97) & Chr(114) & Chr(101),Vbinformation

Now what do you think. The Diassembler will not rip this information due to lack of string information. IF you convert this information manually you will find the String shown above. With the helps of unprotected strings Hackers know how registration message box will appear. If you are good at assembly then you will know this.

3: A OCX A  Day makes Hacker’s good day

Yes, OCX's are the most unprotected stuff you can ever find. It is something which hackers are taken for granted; it’s like bday gift every day. Basically if you are selling OCX's with Nag Screens or with serial key protection then you are the most foolish programmer. OCX'S with Nag Screens are the weakest protection. Using PE Explorer open your OCX and then goto Edit>Resources> there you will find all the Dialog Boxes with everything except code. Now simply delete the nag screen by right clicking; now you won't find any nag screens while running the application.

Ocx's are not easy to protect. Because these are used in design time also. The best way is to compress its resources. Using powerful 3rd party tools like PECompact (http://www.collakesoftware.com )you can protect an OCX. PECompact finds common resources and using some strong compression standards it protects an OCX's and EXE's.

You can also use PECompact to protect your Exe file. PECompact extracts all the resources directly to the memory instead of hard disc during runtime.

4: Protected OCX will not work in design time and also it cannot be registered by regsvr32.exe. But still you can use it or distribute it with your application. By using this method other people cannot use your ocx or view its strings or resources.

If you are using an ocx with your application, use the Unprotected OCX in the design time environment. But when distributing the application compress the ocx with PECompact application and then create a setup program. With ocx in it. But Ocx needs to be registered and the program won't work without ocx. Here's what you got to do.

Goto registry editor, type the name of the ocx and once you find it. Export it to *.reg file. Now tell the installer to put the information in the *.reg file on the target system.

Example: of the *.reg File

[HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\CLSID\{C124B2F-B389-4602-95D1-2C9A42B2A510}\InprocServer32]
@="D:\\Windows\\System32\\Sample Project\\Textbox.ocx"
"ThreadingModel"="Apartment"

You are manually putting the OCX registration. Because you cannot do it with Regsevr32.exe.The only thing you need to do is to change the location of the ocx like %Windir%\System32\textbox.ocx.

Now your ocx is really protected you only know how to use it in your applications.

9: Debuggers

The debuggers are the ultimate weapons for crackers. They use debuggers to pause your application at an API call. Like if you are using Nag Screens for your OCX. Hackers use breakpoints at the Showwindow() Api Call. This will help hackers to look at the HEX Location of your application from the debugger. After that they can modify the exe file at the HEX location. The same way is used to get the Registration Key.

There are lots of tools available to stop debuggers from hacking your application. You can download VB source code for debugger detection here. This is not an advanced detection but still a good one.

The Armadillo software protection mentioned above also uses debugger detection.

The psapi.dll is sometimes used by the debuggers. Make sure this dll is not in use during the execution of your application. You can write a small code on it.

15: Why trial by date is insecure?

As an analyst I can say this is insecure because the software always checks for the system date for retrieving its current trial status. Any hacker can bypass this check by adding breakpoints on the System date api call.

By changing the date the software detects that you have changed the date. But keeping the same date everyday can make most (55 %) of the software work.

Use trial by executions. Its reasonably good.
